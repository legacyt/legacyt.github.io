19Feb.2 Loyalty Programs and the Economy

Background

I dont like loyalty programs. Beyond my general aversion to being monetized and manipulated, there are some more general negative economic outcomes that could result from loyalty programs.

Questions

1.       Do loyalty programs work? Do they increase loyalty or spending?

2.       What, if any, impact do loyalty programs have on the American economy?

Research/Thoughts

Do loyalty programs work? Answer, yes, they do. In the airline industry, they increase (bought) ticket price, but reduce profits, presumably by providing free flights or other benefits to consumers.

Concern: If they work and increase brand loyalty, does this limit the ability of entrants to the market? Is this a problem? Suppose we banned loyalty programs. What would be the effect? Seems like it would be either better service or better prices and in either case, a more competitive market. It would break the subsidies employers pay to travelers through these loyalty programs, potentially making Americans less willing to travel for work. Hard to imagine that's a real issue though - private employers could pay that subsidy directly if it was.

note: amazon prime is a membership I guess, but it's basically the same thing. Sort of. In the same way that any CC with an annual fee is. I can see banning amazon prime. I can't see banning credit cards with annual fees, but I can't think of a justification for the distinction. idk.

Coupons distributed at check out have some similarities to a loyalty program. Give you an incentive to come back tomorrow. Have to figure out how REI fits in. Feels like a loyalty program in that they give you a bit of money to draw you back in. And give a member discount. But no tiers, not really any disincentive from shopping elsewhere also. I suppose thats true at Kroger as well, other than loyalty programs are a pain in the ass and I avoid places that have them. Walmart doesnt, Publix doesnt. I should at Publix specifically because I dont have to fuck with those programs there. Target doesnt push one, but they do have a CC that gives a regular discount. I dont know if having folks use their branded card actually reduces their processing costs or if its just a coupon.

To some extent loyalty programs are a way of reaching price sensitive customers lowering prices for those willing to waste time dealing with the program while maintaining higher profits on folks who value their time more.

Sources

There are many papers that examine the effectiveness of loyalty programs. Ive not found one that examines their broader impact on the health of the economy.

 

19Feb.1 Personality of a tattoo

Questions

1.       Are there substantial differences between folks who have tattoos and those who dont, particularly in personality?

Research

a)       Significantly more forensic psychiatric inpatients with tattoos had a diagnosis of ASPD [anti-social personality disorder] compared to patients without tattoos. Patients with ASPD also had a significantly greater number of tattoos, a trend toward having a greater percentage of their total body surface area tattooed, and were more likely to have a history of substance abuse than patients without ASPD. Tattooed subjects, with or without ASPD, were significantly more likely to have histories of substance abuse, sexual abuse and suicide attempts than non‐tattooed patients.

b)      People with tattoos scored higher on need for uniqueness than non-tattooed individuals.

c)       Men and women ages 2039 were most likely to have been tattooed, as were men with lower levels of education, tradesmen, and women with live-out partners. Tattooing was also associated with risk-taking behaviours, including smoking, greater numbers of lifetime sexual partners, cannabis use (women only) and ever having depression (men only).

d)      There were very few differences in the childhood experiences or personality characteristics of people with or without body modifications. Although people with body modifications did not differ from people without modifications on the Big Five personality measures, people without modifications perceived people with modifications as much different from themselves on these measures. These results indicate that tattoos and piercings in college students are associated with significantly more risk-taking behavior, greater use of alcohol and marijuana, and less social conformity.

e)      compared with non-tattooed (n = 420) individuals, tattooed participants (n = 120) had significantly higher scores on Extraversion, Experience Seeking, Need for Uniqueness, and held more positive Attitudes Toward Tattoos, although effect sizes of these group differences were generally small- to medium-sized.

f)        Women with tattoos were more likely to be younger, to drink more alcohol, to have more psychiatric symptoms and to show borderline personality features than were the non tattooed women. They were also more likely to report CSA.

g)       prior research often suggests that individuals with tattoos commit more crime, are disproportionately concentrated in offender and institutionalized populations, and often have personality disorders.

h)      tattooed adults had significantly higher reactive rebelliousness, anger, and verbal aggression than non-tattooed adults. However, effect sizes were small and there were also no significant between-group differences in terms of proactive rebelliousness, physical aggression, and hostility.

Sources

a)       Tattoos and antisocial personality disorder; Personality and Mental Health, 2008

b)      Tattoos and piercings: Bodily expressions of uniqueness?; Body Image, 2011

c)       Who Gets Tattoos? Demographic and Behavioral Correlates; Annals of Epidemiology, 2012

d)      College Students with Tattoos and Piercings; Psychological Reports, 2001

e)      Personality Differences between Tattooed and Non-Tattooed; Psychological Reports, 2012

f)        Tattoos, childhood sexual abuse and adult psychiatric disorder in women; Womens Mental Health, 1998

g)       Inked into Crime?; Journal of Criminal Justice, 2014

h)      Are Tattooed adults really more aggressive?; Body Image, 2015

 

19Jan.7 Economics of Progeny

Background

I read an interesting article (that I didnt entirely agree with) that raised some interesting points regarding the economics of parenting. The relevant point here was that birth rates have fallen because children are now financial liabilities rather than assets. Children require a lot of inputs and return little value to the household these days, at least in the US, so rational adults are disincentivized from having kids.

Questions

1.       Birthrate varies with race, age, marital status, location etc. Does the fiscal value of a child to the parents explain these differences?

2.       What would be the effect of giving parents direct rights to a percentage of their childrens lifetime earnings?

Research/Thoughts

Regarding question 2, to some extent, parents already claim a portion of the future generations earnings through social programs theyll draw on in retirement. As it is, I have the same access to those programs whether I have children or not (though I am financially contributing to their education and other community programs in support of their generation). Making those claims on future earnings dependent on my personal fertility would presumably increase my willingness to have children. The real question is how much of a kickback would be required to boost the birthrate by a given amount. Which demographics would be most sensitive to this incentive? What effect would this have on the treatment of children? I can see situations where at some point, a parent would want to write off the kid as a loss, but I suppose that loss of future economic value just reverts the kid to what theyre worth today. Could make the parents partially responsible for the financial or even criminal failings of their children as well. Rather unamerican, but putting a parent in prison for a few percent of their kids sentence might incentivize better parenting and better family support mechanisms or lower birth rates in situations where future children are more likely to wind up in prison.

Of course, if we wanted to increase the birth rate, we could also explicitly pay people of our choosing to have kids or punish people who dont have kids. Or we could continue to import children and workers like we do now.

Sources

2013 National Vital Statistics Report, https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/data/nvsr/nvsr64/nvsr64_01.pdf

Starting article: https://qz.com/231313/children-arent-worth-very-much-thats-why-we-no-longer-make-many/

 

19Jan.6 Boney Vegans

Background/Question

Cow milk has a lot of calcium in it. Plants dont have a lot of calcium in them, however the grasses and forbs that ungulates eat tend to be low on digestible energy as well so the animals eat a lot of plant material which makes hitting the needed calcium levels more plausible. Vegans likely consume much less volume of plant than a typical ungulate, so how do they meet their calcium need?

Research/Thoughts

After reviewing a number of informal websites discussing good calcium sources for vegans, it appears that vegans rely on calcium fortified foods to get by. Calcium appears to be one of the more difficult nutrients to acquire with a vegan diet.

19Jan.3 New York Housing and Income

Background

Ive heard a lot about New York Citys lack of affordable housing recently. New York also increased their minimum wage recently.

Question

1.       What will be the impact of increasing the cities minimum wage on the availability of affordable housing?

Thoughts

Increasing the minimum wage will reduce the number of jobs available the bottom of the pay scale. This should drive some low-income people out of New York City because their jobs will be permanently eliminated. That would reduce demand for affordable housing. Simultaneously, those who were able to retain minimum wage jobs would be in a better position to acquire housing that isnt explicitly affordable. Its a convenient solution for the city the minimum wage increase boosts household income in the city, reduces pressure on affordable housing and drives off the poor who arent employable at $15/hr. The city will pay for it with slightly higher prices and reduced economic efficiency. Best case scenario for them would include a revolving door where they are able to import better quality minimum wage workers and eject folks who are liabilities to the city. Their international name recognition may make this revolving door more feasible (fed by immigrants) than it would be in other cities.

In practice the effect would be more complicated. The increase in minimum wage will reduce the number of jobs available, but would also incentivize people to stick around drawing unemployment or just bumming around in hopes of securing a relatively high paying minimum wage job vs looking for options elsewhere which are relatively less valuable. May even draw in people from the surrounding area.

Originally, I hoped to quantify the effect of job loss vs minimum wage and correlate that with current housing shortfall in the city to see what minimum wage would drive out enough low income earners to balance the available housing, but I think the real system is too complicated to draw any useful insights from such a simple correlation.

19Jan.1 CPR Efficacy

Questions

1.       Is CPR an effective way of keeping people alive?

Research/Thoughts

The answer is mostly no, CPR is not effective. In fact, its much less effective than I expected given how much effort is put into training employees how to perform it. Something like 95% of people who need CPR end up dying before they exit the hospital. CPR is better than nothing, but not by much. In adults, Id likely do chest compressions only as its nearly as effective, doesnt put me at risk, and it takes off some of the mental load in an already stressful situation. In children and in cases where drug overdose is likely, rescue breathing becomes important.

Sources

Its a well-documented topic, but I didnt document my reading.

18Dec.1 Urushiol Treatment

Background

Im quite sensitive to urushiol and the related compounds produced by the Anacardiaceae family of plants (cashews, mangos, pistachios, poison oak). Ive been exposed to poison ivy several times in my current job and Ive had to deal with serious rashes.

Question

1.       Whats the best treatment for an urushiol induced rash?

Research/Thoughts

Zanfel is the best topical treatment at this point. Its some kind of detergent mixed with plastic beads that is able to bond to and remove the urushiol after it has attached to the skin. The patent is an interesting read for once. The stuff is 30x more satisfying to use than hydrocortisone, which is probably the second most effective topical treatment. Technu may also be able to remove some of the oil after it has attached to the skin, but its performance isnt great it wasnt designed with urushiol in mind. Prednisone/Prednisolone is a corticosteroid that taken internally and is supposed to work well though Ive not had to use it yet. The packaging for Zanfel recommends Prednisone for systemic cases of poison ivy.

Sources

The patent for Zanfel: https://patents.google.com/patent/US6423746B1/
